# Presentation Asset Relationship Map

**Version:** v1.0 (Frozen Baseline)

---

# Purpose

This document describes the structural relationships among the research assets generated during the Presentation Development Project.

Whereas the Asset Inventory identifies individual assets and the Asset Classification organizes them according to functional roles, this document explains how these assets collectively formed an integrated operational capability.

The objective is to reveal the internal structure of the Presentation Project as a coherent research asset rather than as a collection of independent deliverables.

---

# Relationship Principle

The Presentation Development Project did not produce isolated documents.

Instead, multiple categories of research assets gradually became interconnected through repeated design, dialogue, operational refinement, and architectural integration.

The relationships described below therefore represent functional dependencies rather than chronological development.

# Relationship Overview

## Presentation Assets

Presentation assets provide the primary interface between the Research Program and external audiences.

They serve as the central communication layer from which several additional asset categories emerged.

---

## Visual Assets

Visual assets enhance structural understanding.

The Master Visual Language, Meta Visual, and Research Architecture figures support both presentation activities and architectural explanation.

Visual assets therefore extend beyond presentation itself and become reusable explanatory resources.

---

## Dialogue Assets

Dialogue assets enable collaborative exploration with external audiences.

Rather than functioning as presentation scripts alone, they facilitate comparative discussion, operational observation, and shared understanding.

---

## Methodological Assets

Methodological assets standardize the presentation development process.

These assets transform repeated presentation design into reusable methods that can support future projects.

---

## Operational Assets

Operational assets support practical execution.

Meeting templates, observation records, campaign reports, and operational reviews enable the Presentation Project to function as an ongoing operational activity.

---

## Protocol Assets

Protocol assets establish standardized operational procedures.

They connect presentation activities with organizational continuity by defining repeatable practices for preparation, dialogue, observation, and review.

---

## Architectural Assets

Architectural assets represent the highest level of integration.

The Presentation Framework, Operational Research Program Architecture, and Research Program Operational Cycle position the Presentation Project within the broader Research Program.

---

# Structural Interpretation

The relationships among these assets demonstrate that the Presentation Development Project evolved from a communication activity into an integrated operational capability.

Rather than existing independently, each asset category supports the formation of the next architectural layer.

The resulting structure therefore represents an interconnected research asset capable of supporting long-term collaboration, operational continuity, and future methodological development.
