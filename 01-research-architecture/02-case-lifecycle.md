# Case Lifecycle

This document describes how observations move through the research process within the Dialogue-Phase Reasoning program.

The goal is not to prescribe a fixed workflow, but to describe recurring patterns observed throughout long-horizon dialogue analysis.

---

# Core Principle

A dialogue does not automatically become a research observation.

Likewise, a Case does not automatically become a theory.

Between dialogue and theory, multiple layers of observation, comparison, and reorganization may occur.

The Case Lifecycle describes these intermediate transformations.

---

# Lifecycle Overview

```text
Dialogue

↓

Observation

↓

Case

↓

↓

Research Note

↓

↓

```

---

# Stage 1 — Dialogue

Dialogue functions as the primary observational environment.

Potential observations emerge through interaction, but remain unstructured.

At this stage:

* phenomena are observed
* patterns are noticed
* interpretations remain tentative

---

# Stage 2 — Observation

Particular events, transitions, or structures are identified as potentially significant.

Examples may include:

* Jump-like transitions
* Crystallization processes
* Externalization processes

Observation alone does not yet create a Case.

---

# Stage 3 — Case

An observation becomes a Case when it is documented and preserved.

The purpose of a Case is:

* observation preservation
* future comparison
* reproducibility of interpretation

Cases remain local observations.

---

# Stage 4 — Selected Case

Some Cases become Selected Cases.

Selection occurs when an observation appears:

* unusually clear
* recurrent
* structurally significant
* useful for future comparison

Selected Cases become part of the long-term observational corpus.

---

# Stage 5 — Comparative Review

Multiple Cases are examined together.

The focus shifts from:

individual phenomena

toward

relationships among phenomena.

At this stage:

* similarities emerge
* differences emerge
* recurring patterns become visible

---

# Stage 6 — Cluster Formation

Cases begin forming provisional clusters.

Examples:

* Crystallization-dominant Cases
* Externalization-dominant Cases
* Mixed Cases
* Transitional Cases

Clusters remain observational rather than theoretical.

---

# Stage 7 — Open Questions

Comparative analysis frequently generates questions that cannot yet be resolved.

Examples:

* Why are Externalization Cases increasing?
* What distinguishes stabilization from projection?
* How should mixed Cases be interpreted?

Open Questions preserve uncertainty rather than forcing conclusions.

---

# Stage 8 — Meta Cases

Meta Cases emerge when the object of observation shifts.

Instead of observing:

individual Cases

the focus becomes:

* Case structures
* Cluster dynamics
* Open Question generation
* Research-space evolution

Meta Cases therefore observe the observation process itself.

Example:

Case Recovery

↓

Cluster Review

↓

Open Question Formation

↓

Research-Space Reorganization

---

# Stage 9 — Research Notes

Research Notes preserve broader reflections that extend beyond individual Cases.

These may include:

* structural interpretations
* research directions
* organizational insights
* methodological considerations

Research Notes remain exploratory.

---

# Stage 10 — Theory Skeleton

When multiple observations converge, provisional theoretical structures may emerge.

Theory Skeletons are not final theories.

They function as:

* organizational frameworks
* conceptual scaffolds
* future research candidates

Case34 provides an example of this pathway.

---

# Stage 11 — Research Program

A mature research program may emerge from repeated cycles of:

Observation

↓

Case

↓

Comparison

↓

Meta Observation

↓

Theory Formation

↓

Further Observation

At this stage, the research environment itself becomes part of the observational process.

---

# Position of Meta Cases

Meta Cases do not belong to the same category as:

* Jump
* Crystallization
* Externalization

These are observed phenomena.

Meta Cases instead belong to the observation layer itself.

They document how observations are organized, compared, and transformed into research structures.

---

# One-Line Summary

The Case Lifecycle describes how dialogue observations evolve from local Cases into comparative structures, Meta Cases, research notes, and eventually theoretical frameworks.
