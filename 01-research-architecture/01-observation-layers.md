# Observation Layers

This document describes the primary observational layers currently used within the Dialogue-Phase Reasoning research program.

The purpose of these layers is not to provide theoretical definitions, but to organize recurring types of observable semantic transitions.

---

# Observation Principle

The project treats dialogue as an observational environment.

Within that environment, certain recurring structural phenomena appear repeatedly across different interactions.

Observation layers provide a provisional framework for organizing those phenomena.

---

# Current Observation Layers

```text
Dialogue

↓

10-Jump

20-Crystallization

30-Externalization
```

These layers are observational categories rather than fixed theoretical claims.

---

# 10-Jump

## General Description

The Jump layer concerns observations of qualitative semantic transitions.

Typical observations include:

* abrupt structural reorganization
* discontinuous reinterpretation
* unexpected shifts in explanatory frameworks
* transitions difficult to explain through incremental accumulation alone

---

## Typical Questions

* What conditions precede a Jump?
* Are Jumps gradual or discontinuous?
* Can Jump-like transitions be recognized retrospectively?

---

# 20-Crystallization

## General Description

The Crystallization layer concerns observations of semantic stabilization.

Typical observations include:

* structural integration
* conceptual consolidation
* increasing explanatory coherence
* stabilization of previously fragmented elements

---

## Typical Characteristics

Observed patterns often include:

* accumulation
* connection
* reorganization
* stabilization

Crystallization generally reduces ambiguity and increases structural coherence.

---

## Typical Questions

* What promotes stabilization?
* How stable are crystallized structures over time?
* Can crystallization be measured operationally?

---

# 30-Externalization

## General Description

The Externalization layer concerns observations of semantic projection into external structures.

Typical observations include:

* README formation
* repository topology development
* navigation-layer construction
* research-space organization
* external shell formation

---

## Typical Characteristics

Observed patterns often include:

* structure-preserving projection
* low-loss external representation
* navigation-oriented organization
* semantic topology formation

Externalization focuses on how internal structures become publicly observable and reusable.

---

## Typical Questions

* What enables low-loss projection?
* How does topology affect interpretation?
* What role does navigation play in externalization?

---

# Relationship Between Layers

The layers should not necessarily be interpreted as strictly sequential.

Observed interactions may include:

```text
20 → 30

30 → 20

10 → 20

10 → 30
```

Different phenomena may interact across layers.

The project therefore treats the layers as observational domains rather than rigid stages.

---

# Current Research Position

At present:

* 10-Jump focuses on qualitative transitions
* 20-Crystallization focuses on stabilization
* 30-Externalization focuses on projection

Future observations may refine, merge, split, or reorganize these categories.

The framework remains observational and provisional.

---

# One-Line Summary

Observation Layers organize recurring semantic phenomena observed in long-horizon dialogue environments, including transition, stabilization, and external projection processes.
