# Research Program Evolution

This document explores a possible long-term direction emerging from the current Dialogue-Phase Reasoning research program.

The purpose is not to predict future outcomes, but to describe an observed sequence of organizational developments that appear to be gradually forming across multiple research layers.

---

# Current Development Sequence

Repeated observations suggest the following provisional trajectory:

```text
Dialogue
↓
Concept Emergence
↓
Concept Index
↓
Knowledge Graph Formation
↓
Research Architecture Formation
↓
Research Space Evolution
↓
Adaptive Research Ecology
↓
Self-Renewing Research Program
```

The significance of this sequence is that the primary object of organization gradually shifts:

from individual concepts,

to relationships between concepts,

to the architecture of knowledge,

and eventually to the research environment itself.

---

# Research Interpretation

At the present stage, the project does not focus on the creation of a single model, theory, or artifact.

Instead, it increasingly investigates how observations, concepts, structures, repositories, and organizational processes interact over time.

In this sense, the research program gradually expands from:

knowledge generation

toward

knowledge-environment formation.

---

```text
Knowledge Storage
```

---

# Footnote: Relationship to AGI Research

This framework should not be interpreted as an alternative to, or replacement for, current AGI research programs.

Rather, it addresses a different level of analysis.

Many AGI-oriented efforts primarily investigate:

* model capability
* reasoning performance
* planning
* agency
* autonomous task execution

The present framework focuses instead on:

* concept formation
* knowledge organization
* research-space formation
* long-horizon structural evolution

Accordingly, the two directions may be viewed as complementary rather than competing.

From this perspective, AGI can be interpreted as one possible development occurring within a broader knowledge and research ecosystem, rather than as the sole endpoint of research evolution.

This interpretation remains provisional and is included only as an orientation note.
