# Conversation Design Methodology

## Harvest Development Methodology

Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document summarizes the Conversation Design Methodology developed within Harvest Development.

The objective is not to explain the Research Program itself.

Instead, this methodology describes how conversations can gradually connect organizations to the developmental architecture of the Research Program through comparison, reflection, and collaborative understanding.

Conversation is therefore treated as an architectural process rather than a communication technique.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Conversation Design Methodology is based on one central principle.

**Organizations should not be taught the Research Program.**

Instead,

organizations should gradually recognize similarities between their own operational experiences and the phenomena previously observed within the Research Program.

Understanding emerges through comparison rather than explanation.

---

# Core Principle

The methodology preserves the following developmental order.

Shared Experience

↓

Observable Phenomenon

↓

Comparison

↓

Recognition

↓

Reflection

↓

Emerging Curiosity

↓

Supporting Evidence

↓

Research Program

Each stage prepares the conditions for the next stage.

The sequence should never be reversed.

---

# Methodological Principles

## 1. Phenomenon First

Conversation always begins from observable operational experiences.

Research concepts are intentionally postponed.

Participants first discuss their own reality.

---

## 2. Comparison Before Explanation

Facilitators avoid explaining conclusions.

Instead, they encourage participants to compare experiences.

Comparison naturally generates recognition.

---

## 3. Reflection Before Evidence

Reflection creates cognitive ownership.

Supporting evidence is introduced only after participants have begun constructing their own observations.

---

## 4. Curiosity Before Research Program

The Research Program should never initiate the conversation.

Instead, it appears as one possible response to questions that have already emerged through dialogue.

---

## 5. Invitation Rather Than Persuasion

Conversation never attempts to convince participants.

Its purpose is to invite collaborative exploration.

Organizations remain active participants throughout the dialogue.

---

# Conversation Architecture

The methodology is implemented through a staged conversation sequence.

| Stage | Primary Objective |
|--------|-------------------|
| Shared Workplace Experience | Establish common ground |
| Observable Phenomenon | Recognize operational reality |
| Comparison | Compare experiences |
| Recognition | Identify similarities and differences |
| Reflection | Encourage participant-generated observations |
| Emerging Curiosity | Preserve open questions |
| Supporting Evidence | Introduce relevant research assets |
| Research Program | Present the broader developmental framework |

Each stage supports cognitive development rather than information transfer.

---

# Facilitator Role

The facilitator does not function as a lecturer.

Instead, the facilitator serves as:

- Conversation guide
- Comparative observer
- Reflection supporter
- Curiosity facilitator

The facilitator avoids providing premature conclusions.

---

# Audience Role

Participants are not passive listeners.

They actively contribute by:

- Sharing operational experiences
- Comparing observations
- Reflecting on differences
- Identifying emerging questions
- Constructing shared understanding

Conversation is therefore co-created.

---

# Relationship with Visual Architecture

Conversation Design defines the developmental structure.

Visual Conversation Architecture supports that structure.

Visuals therefore strengthen conversation but never replace it.

Conversation

↓

Visual Support

↓

Shared Understanding

---

# Relationship with Demonstration Design

Conversation Design defines the cognitive architecture.

Demonstration Design transforms that architecture into a presentation experience.

Conversation Design

↓

Visual Architecture

↓

Demonstration Script

↓

Audience Cognitive Journey

Each layer preserves the developmental order established by Conversation Design.

---

# Success Criteria

The conversation is considered successful when participants naturally progress through the following sequence.

"I recognize this."

↓

"We have experienced something similar."

↓

"I had not thought about it that way."

↓

"Why does this happen?"

↓

"Can we explore this together?"

At this point, the Research Program can be introduced as a natural continuation of the dialogue.
