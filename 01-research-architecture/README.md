# Research Architecture

This section describes the conceptual architecture of the Dialogue-Phase Reasoning research program.

Unlike repository topology, which organizes files and navigation paths, the Research Architecture layer organizes the relationship between:

* dialogue
* observed phenomena
* case observations
* comparative analysis
* research mapping
* research-space evolution

The goal is to clarify what is being observed, how observations are organized, and how research structures emerge over time.

# Core Principle

The project does not treat documents, repositories, or theories as primary objects.

Instead, the primary object of observation is:

Human–AI Dialogue

and the semantic phenomena that emerge through long-horizon interaction.

# Layer Descriptions

## Level 0 — Dialogue

The source environment in which observations occur.

Dialogue is treated as the observational field rather than merely a communication channel.

---

## Level 1 — Observed Phenomena

This layer contains the primary categories of observed semantic transitions.

Current observational domains include:

* 10-Jump
* 20-Crystallization
* 30-Externalization

These categories describe observed phenomena rather than theoretical conclusions.

---

## Level 2 — Case Observation

Individual observations are preserved as Case records.

Selected Cases represent observations judged sufficiently stable or illustrative for long-term reference.

---

## Level 3 — Comparative Observation

Multiple Cases are compared in order to identify recurring patterns, distinctions, and unresolved ambiguities.

This layer includes:

* comparative reviews
* cluster observations
* cross-case analysis

---

## Level 4 — Research Mapping

Comparative observations often generate new questions rather than final answers.

This layer preserves:

* Open Questions
* Meta Cases
* unresolved research directions

Meta Cases differ from ordinary Cases because they observe Case structures themselves rather than individual phenomena.

---

## Level 5 — Research-Space Evolution

At this level, observation begins influencing the organization of the research environment itself.

Examples include:

* repository topology
* navigation architecture
* research-space reconstruction
* observation-driven structural evolution

---

# Current Position of Meta Cases

Meta Cases occupy a unique position within the research architecture.

They do not primarily observe dialogue phenomena.

Instead, they observe:

Case

↓

Case Comparison

↓

Cluster Formation

↓

Open Question Generation

↓

Research-Space Reorganization

For this reason, Meta Cases are placed within the Research Mapping layer rather than within the individual phenomenon layers.

---

# One-Line Summary

The Research Architecture layer describes how dialogue observations evolve into Cases, how Cases evolve into research structures, and how research structures gradually reshape the research space itself.
