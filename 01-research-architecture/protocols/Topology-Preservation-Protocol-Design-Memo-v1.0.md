## Purpose

This document records the design rationale behind **Topology Preservation Protocol v1.0**.

Its purpose is not to explain the complete methodological background.

Instead, it summarizes the essential design decisions that shaped the operational protocol.

This document serves as a reference for future revisions of the protocol.

---

## Design Objectives

The protocol was designed to satisfy the following objectives:

- Preserve structural organization across representational transformations.
- Maintain operational simplicity.
- Maximize reproducibility.
- Minimize dependence on prior research discussions.
- Provide a reusable workflow for future diagram-generation instances.

---

## Design Principles

The protocol was developed according to the following principles:

- Structure is prioritized over visual appearance.
- Compression must preserve topology.
- Operational procedures should remain simple.
- Every workflow step should have a clear **Objective**, **Input**, **Procedure**, and **Output**.
- The protocol should be executable without requiring knowledge of the research history.

---

## Design Evidence

The protocol was derived from repeated observations of diagram-generation workflows involving:

- detailed diagrams
- compressed diagrams
- presentation diagrams
- publication diagrams

Across these observations, structural preservation consistently proved more important than visual similarity.

These recurring observations became the primary design evidence for the protocol.

---

## Methodological Scope

Extensive methodological discussions preceded the protocol.

These discussions included topics such as:

- structural observation
- topology preservation
- observation criteria
- workflow organization

However, these methodological developments are treated as supporting background rather than part of the operational protocol.

Version 1.0 intentionally emphasizes implementation rather than theoretical completeness.

---

## Version 1.0 Policy

Version 1.0 intentionally adopts a conservative design.

The protocol includes only procedures considered essential for operational use.

Additional methodological refinements, decision gates, and advanced validation procedures are reserved for future versions if operational experience demonstrates their necessity.

---

## Future Development

Possible future improvements include:

- automated topology verification
- decision gates for complex workflows
- protocol variants for different diagram types
- quantitative validation metrics
- integration with future diagram-generation systems

Future extensions should preserve the simplicity of Version 1.0 whenever possible.

## Concluding Note

Topology Preservation Protocol v1.0 represents the operationalization of accumulated structural observations.

The protocol is intended to function as a reusable operational asset rather than a theoretical framework.

Future revisions should prioritize improvements in:

- usability
- reproducibility
- operational clarity

while preserving the protocol's structural simplicity.
