# Placement Design Memo v1.0

## Purpose

This document records the design rationale behind Placement Protocol v1.0.

Its purpose is not to explain the complete organizational background of the Placement Principle.

Instead, it summarizes the essential design decisions that shaped the operational protocol.

This document serves as a reference for future revisions of the protocol.

---

## Design Objectives

The protocol was designed to satisfy the following objectives:

- standardize repository placement procedures,
- preserve organizational consistency,
- maximize reproducibility,
- support long-term repository maintenance,
- provide a reusable operational procedure for future repository operations.

---

## Design Principles

The protocol was developed according to the following principles:

- organizational role precedes repository placement,
- placement decisions should follow accumulated organizational observations,
- repository consistency is prioritized over individual preference,
- operational procedures should remain simple,
- every workflow step should contain a clear objective, input, procedure, and output.

---

## Design Evidence

The protocol was derived from repeated repository placement activities involving:

- research architecture documents,
- methodological documents,
- Meta Cases,
- research notes,
- operational assets,
- cross-repository relationships.

Across these observations, organizational consistency consistently proved more important than individual placement decisions.

These recurring operational observations became the design evidence for the protocol.

---

## Operational Scope

Placement Protocol v1.0 focuses on operational implementation.

The protocol does not redefine the Placement Principle.

Instead, it provides a standardized operational procedure for applying the accumulated Placement Principle consistently during repository operations.

---

## Version 1.0 Policy

Version 1.0 intentionally adopts a conservative design.

The protocol includes only procedures considered essential for consistent repository placement.

Additional operational refinements and specialized placement procedures are reserved for future versions if operational experience demonstrates their necessity.

---

## Future Development

Possible future improvements include:

- protocol variants for different repository operations,
- operational decision support,
- repository validation assistance,
- automated consistency verification,
- integration with future repository management workflows.

Future extensions should preserve the simplicity of Version 1.0 whenever possible.

---

## Concluding Note

Placement Protocol v1.0 represents the operationalization of the accumulated Placement Principle.

The protocol is intended to function as a reusable operational asset rather than as an organizational theory.

Future revisions should prioritize operational clarity, reproducibility, and long-term maintainability while preserving the simplicity of Version 1.0.
