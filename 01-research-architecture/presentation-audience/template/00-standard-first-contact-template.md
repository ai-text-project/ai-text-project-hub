# Standard First Contact Template v1.0

## Purpose

This template provides a standard first-contact message for introducing the Human–AI Collaboration Research Program to enterprise organizations.

Its purpose is not to explain the entire Research Program in an email.

Instead, the email serves as an entry point that invites the recipient to explore the GitHub repository and, if interested, begin a comparative dialogue.

---

# Subject

Research Introduction: Human–AI Collaboration Research Program

---

# Email Template

Dear [Recipient Name],

I hope this message finds you well.

My name is [Your Name], and I am conducting an independent research program on long-term Human–AI Collaboration.

Rather than presenting research findings in a one-way manner, this Research Program explores how researchers and enterprise practitioners can learn from each other through comparative dialogue based on their respective experiences.

To make the Research Program easy to review, I have prepared a GitHub repository that serves as the entry point to the project.

GitHub Repository

[Repository URL]

For first-time visitors, I recommend the following reading sequence:

1. README
2. Executive Summary
3. Meta Visual
4. Presentation Guide
5. Presentation

This sequence provides a gradual introduction to the Research Program, from its overall purpose to the presentation materials.

If you find the Research Program relevant to your interests, I would be grateful for the opportunity to have a short online meeting to exchange perspectives.

The purpose of the meeting is not to deliver a formal presentation, but to engage in a comparative dialogue regarding Human–AI Collaboration and practical experience in enterprise environments.

Thank you very much for your time and consideration.

Kind regards,

[Your Name]

## Success Criteria

The objective of this email is not immediate collaboration.

A successful first contact is one that enables the recipient to understand the Research Program and leaves the door open for future dialogue.

Long-term trust and sustained communication are considered more valuable than immediate outcomes.
