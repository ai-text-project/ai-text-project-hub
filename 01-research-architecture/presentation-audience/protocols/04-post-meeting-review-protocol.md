# Post-Meeting Review Protocol

**Protocol Version:** v1.0 (Provisional)

---

# Purpose

This protocol defines the standard post-meeting review process following enterprise engagement.

Its objective is not to evaluate meeting success in commercial terms, but to capture observations, reflections, and emerging insights that contribute to the continuous evolution of the Research Program.

Each meeting is treated as an opportunity for mutual learning and operational refinement.

---

# Scope

This protocol applies after:

- Initial enterprise meetings
- Research demonstrations
- Follow-up discussions
- Collaborative exploration sessions

The protocol begins immediately after the meeting concludes.

---

# Review Principles

Post-meeting reviews follow five guiding principles.

## 1. Record Before Interpretation

Capture observations while they remain fresh.

Avoid immediate conclusions or strategic judgments.

---

## 2. Preserve Evidence

Record concrete observations rather than impressions whenever possible.

Observed dialogue is more valuable than retrospective assumptions.

---

## 3. Separate Observation from Reflection

Observation records and personal reflections should remain distinguishable.

This separation supports future comparative analysis.

---

## 4. Continuous Improvement

Each meeting contributes to improving:

- Presentation Packages
- Dialogue Structure
- Communication Materials
- Operational Procedures

---

## 5. Research Continuity

Meeting outcomes become part of the long-term Research Program rather than isolated meeting records.

---

# Standard Review Workflow

```text
Meeting Completed
        │
        ▼
Observation Record
        │
        ▼
Reflection Memo
        │
        ▼
Operational Findings
        │
        ▼
Campaign Database
        │
        ▼
Comparative Review
```

Each review contributes to cumulative organizational learning.

---

# Step 1

Observation Record

Immediately document factual observations.

Typical items include:

- Meeting participants
- Topics discussed
- Questions raised
- Audience reactions
- Requests for clarification
- Collaboration interests

The objective is to preserve observable evidence.

---

# Step 2

Reflection Memo

Review the meeting from the Research Program perspective.

Suggested questions include:

- What worked particularly well?
- Which explanations required additional clarification?
- Which visual materials proved effective?
- Which assumptions should be reconsidered?

Reflection should remain exploratory rather than judgmental.

---

# Step 3

Operational Findings

Identify operational improvements.

Examples include:

- Navigation improvements
- Presentation adjustments
- Dialogue refinements
- Supporting material updates
- Communication sequencing

Operational findings support future protocol refinement.

---

# Step 4

Campaign Database

Register reusable observations within the campaign records.

Possible categories include:

- Audience Characteristics
- Frequently Asked Questions
- Successful Dialogue Patterns
- Communication Challenges
- Emerging Collaboration Opportunities

Accumulated records enable long-term comparative analysis.

---

# Step 5

Comparative Review

Review multiple meetings collectively.

The objective is to identify:

- recurring audience responses
- stable communication patterns
- repeated operational issues
- emerging strategic opportunities

Comparative Review provides evidence for future protocol revisions.

---

# Review Outputs

Each completed review should generate:

- Observation Record
- Reflection Memo
- Operational Findings
- Campaign Update
- Recommendations (if applicable)

---
