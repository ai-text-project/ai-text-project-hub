# Presentation Development Protocol

**Protocol Version:** v1.0 (Provisional)

---

# Purpose

This protocol defines the standard development process for transforming completed Research Program assets into audience-oriented Presentation Packages.

Its purpose is not simply to produce presentation slides, but to establish a repeatable communication development workflow that preserves the architectural continuity of the Research Program while enabling audience-specific adaptation.

The protocol serves as the master workflow for Presentation Audience development.

---

# Scope

This protocol applies to the development of all Presentation Packages within the Research Program.

Typical examples include:

- Enterprise Presentation
- AI Research Institute Presentation
- Future University Edition
- Government Edition
- Community Edition

The protocol defines the overall development sequence.

Individual operational procedures are documented in subordinate protocols.

---

# Development Principles

Presentation development follows five fundamental principles.

## 1. Research First

Presentations are projections of completed research assets.

Presentation development never precedes research development.

---

## 2. Architecture Preservation

The overall architecture of the Research Program shall remain stable throughout the presentation development process.

Audience adaptation must not alter the underlying research architecture.

---

## 3. Shared Core

Each Presentation Package preserves a common narrative structure representing the Research Program.

This Shared Core provides consistency across multiple audience editions.

---

## 4. Audience-Specific Projection

Audience-specific information is introduced only where necessary.

Different audiences receive different projections while preserving the same architectural foundation.

---

## 5. Progressive Development

Presentation Packages evolve through multiple development stages rather than being produced in a single step.

---

# Standard Development Workflow

The standard workflow consists of seven phases.

```text
Research Assets
        │
        ▼
Presentation Design
        │
        ▼
Presentation Package
        │
        ▼
Presentation Audience Portal
        │
        ▼
Operational Preparation
        │
        ▼
Quality Review
        │
        ▼
Campaign Deployment
```

Each phase produces reusable research assets for subsequent development.
