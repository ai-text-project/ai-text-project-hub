# Campaign Review Protocol

**Protocol Version:** v1.0 (Provisional)

---

# Purpose

This protocol defines the standard review process for enterprise engagement campaigns conducted within the Research Program.

Its objective is to evaluate the campaign as a whole from a research and operational perspective, rather than assessing individual meetings in isolation.

The protocol supports long-term organizational learning by identifying recurring observations, communication patterns, operational improvements, and emerging opportunities across multiple enterprise engagements.

---

# Scope

This protocol applies after a series of enterprise engagements have been completed.

Typical review units include:

- Initial Pilot Campaign
- Industry-specific Campaign
- Research Institute Campaign
- Multi-organization Campaign

Campaign Review should be conducted periodically rather than after every individual meeting.

---

# Review Principles

Campaign Review follows five guiding principles.

## 1. Campaign Before Individual Meetings

Focus on recurring patterns across the campaign rather than isolated observations.

---

## 2. Evidence-Based Review

Review conclusions should be supported by accumulated Observation Records and Reflection Memos.

Avoid relying on individual impressions.

---

## 3. Comparative Interpretation

Compare multiple organizations to identify:

- common interests
- recurring questions
- communication differences
- operational tendencies

---

## 4. Continuous Refinement

Campaign Review should generate recommendations for improving:

- Presentation Packages
- Dialogue Structure
- Operational Protocols
- Observation Methods

---

## 5. Research Program Development

Campaign findings should contribute to the ongoing development of the Research Program.

The campaign itself becomes an object of structural observation.

---

```text
Observation Records
        │
        ▼
Reflection Memos
        │
        ▼
Comparative Analysis
        │
        ▼
Campaign Findings
        │
        ▼
Operational Recommendations
        │
        ▼
Research Program Evolution
```

Campaign Review transforms operational experience into reusable research knowledge.

---

# Step 1

Collect Campaign Evidence

Gather:

- Observation Records
- Reflection Memos
- Meeting Summaries
- Operational Findings

These documents provide the evidence base for review.

---

# Step 2

Comparative Analysis

Review observations collectively.

Possible review questions include:

- Which themes appeared repeatedly?
- Which explanations consistently required clarification?
- Which presentation materials proved most effective?
- Which audience characteristics influenced dialogue?

The objective is to identify stable patterns rather than isolated events.

---

# Step 3

Campaign Findings

Summarize key observations, including:

- recurring audience interests
- communication strengths
- communication challenges
- operational lessons
- collaboration signals

Campaign Findings should remain evidence-based.

---

# Step 4

Operational Recommendations

Identify practical improvements.

Examples include:

- refining Presentation Packages
- improving Executive Summaries
- updating Dialogue Protocols
- enhancing Observation Templates
- revising Campaign Procedures

Recommendations should support future campaigns.

---

# Step 5

Research Program Interpretation

Consider whether the campaign revealed broader developments within the Research Program.

Examples include:

- emergence of reusable communication patterns
- maturation of Presentation Framework
- operational architecture development
- new audience categories
- methodological implications

Significant observations may be preserved as Emergence Notes.

---

# Campaign Outputs

A completed Campaign Review should produce:

- Campaign Review Report
- Operational Recommendations
- Updated Communication Assets (if required)
- Candidate Emergence Notes
- Future Campaign Priorities

---

# Expected Outcomes

Application of this protocol enables:

- evidence-based campaign improvement
- consistent organizational learning
- refinement of communication architecture
- identification of emerging research opportunities
- preservation of strategic knowledge

Campaign Review therefore serves as the bridge between operational experience and long-term Research Program development.
