# Research Audience Initial Contact Protocol v1.0

## Purpose

This protocol defines a standard operational approach for introducing the Human–AI Collaboration Research Program to research institutes, universities, and public research organizations.

Its objective is not to request immediate collaboration.

Instead, it provides a structured entry point that enables research organizations to understand the Research Program and determine the most appropriate internal research group or researcher for future dialogue.

---

# Fundamental Principle

The initial contact should introduce the Research Program as an open research asset rather than as a proposal directed to a specific individual.

The receiving organization is encouraged to review the Research Program and identify the most appropriate internal contact according to its own research structure and expertise.

---

# Operational Objectives

The first contact has four objectives.

1. Introduce the Human–AI Collaboration Research Program.

2. Provide a clear entry point through the GitHub repository.

3. Invite voluntary exploration of the Research Program.

4. Open the possibility of future Research Comparative Dialogue.

Immediate collaboration is not expected.

Long-term research communication is considered the primary objective.

---

# GitHub as the Research Entry Point

The GitHub repository functions as the public entry point to the Research Program.

Rather than explaining the complete Research Program within an email, recipients are invited to navigate the repository using the recommended sequence.

Recommended reading order:

1. README
2. Executive Summary
3. Meta Visual
4. Presentation Guide
5. Presentation

Formation History may be introduced as supplementary material for organizations interested in understanding the developmental evolution of the Research Program.

---

# Initial Email Characteristics

The initial email should be:

- Professional
- Respectful
- Concise
- Non-promotional
- Research-oriented

The email should avoid excessive technical explanation.

Its primary function is to encourage recipients to review the Research Program independently.

---

# Research Comparative Dialogue

If the recipient expresses interest, a short online meeting may be proposed.

The meeting should be positioned as a Research Comparative Dialogue.

Its purpose is not:

- a formal presentation,
- a sales meeting,
- or an immediate collaboration request.

Instead, the dialogue provides an opportunity to compare perspectives, research environments, and practical experiences related to Human–AI Collaboration.

---

# Contact Strategy

The initial contact should normally be directed to an institutional research contact rather than to an individual researcher.

Whenever possible, the receiving organization should determine the most appropriate internal research group or researcher after reviewing the Research Program.

A typical closing sentence may include:

> If the Research Program appears relevant, we would greatly appreciate your advice regarding the most appropriate research group or contact within your organization.

This approach respects the internal organizational structure of each research institution.

---

# Success Criteria

The objective of the first contact is not immediate collaboration.

Success is achieved when:

- the Research Program is reviewed,
- the GitHub repository is explored,
- internal discussion becomes possible,
- or the Research Program is forwarded to an appropriate research group.

Long-term trust and sustainable research communication are considered significantly more valuable than immediate outcomes.

---

# Operational Philosophy

Research collaboration develops through gradual mutual understanding.

The initial email should therefore be regarded as the beginning of a long-term relationship rather than the conclusion of a proposal.

The protocol prioritizes:

- openness,
- transparency,
- voluntary engagement,
- and sustained Research Comparative Dialogue.

These principles provide the operational foundation for connecting independent research programs with research organizations in a respectful and sustainable manner.
