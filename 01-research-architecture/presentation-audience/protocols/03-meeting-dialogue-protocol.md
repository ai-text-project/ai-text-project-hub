# Meeting Dialogue Protocol

**Protocol Version:** v1.0 (Provisional)

---

# Purpose

This protocol defines the standard dialogue structure for enterprise meetings within the Research Program.

Its objective is not to deliver a presentation or persuade an audience.

Instead, the meeting is designed as a collaborative dialogue that enables both parties to develop a shared understanding through observation, comparison, and reflection.

---

# Scope

This protocol applies to:

- Initial enterprise meetings
- Research introduction sessions
- Demonstration meetings
- Follow-up discussions
- Collaborative exploration meetings

The protocol assumes that an initial contact has already been established.

---

# Dialogue Principles

Enterprise dialogue follows five fundamental principles.

## 1. Shared Observation

Begin with experiences that both parties can recognize.

Avoid introducing theoretical concepts before establishing common operational ground.

---

## 2. Progressive Understanding

Move gradually from experience to structure.

Do not introduce complex research concepts before the audience has developed sufficient context.

---

## 3. Comparative Dialogue

Encourage comparison between:

- current organizational practice
- observed operational challenges
- Research Program perspectives

Dialogue should remain exploratory rather than persuasive.

---

## 4. Curiosity Before Explanation

When possible, allow curiosity to emerge naturally before introducing detailed explanations.

Questions often provide better entry points than direct instruction.

---

## 5. Future-Oriented Conversation

Conclude by exploring possible future collaboration rather than attempting to reach immediate agreement.

---

# Standard Dialogue Workflow

```text
Opening
        │
        ▼
Shared Experience
        │
        ▼
Operational Challenge
        │
        ▼
Research Perspective
        │
        ▼
Comparative Dialogue
        │
        ▼
Reflection
        │
        ▼

```

The dialogue should progress naturally without forcing transitions.

---

# Dialogue Stages

## Stage 1

Opening

Objectives:

- establish rapport
- clarify meeting purpose
- introduce the Research Program briefly

---

## Stage 2

Shared Experience

Discuss operational situations familiar to both parties.

The objective is to create common ground.

---

## Stage 3

Operational Challenge

Explore practical challenges experienced by the organization.

Avoid introducing solutions too early.

---

## Stage 4

Research Perspective

Present relevant observations from the Research Program.

Explain how similar operational situations have been studied.

---

## Stage 5

Comparative Dialogue

Encourage comparison between:

- enterprise experience
- research observations
- possible similarities
- possible differences

Dialogue should remain open-ended.

---

## Stage 6

Reflection

Invite participants to reflect on:

- unexpected insights
- interesting observations
- potential implications

Reflection supports deeper understanding.

---

## Stage 7

Future Possibilities

Discuss possible next steps, including:

- additional conversations
- information sharing
- collaborative exploration
- future meetings

The objective is to leave the conversation open for continued dialogue.

---

# Dialogue Characteristics

Effective dialogue should be:

- conversational
- observation-oriented
- respectful
- exploratory
- evidence-based
- adaptable

The conversation should avoid excessive technical explanation unless requested.

---

# Supporting Materials

Typical supporting materials include:

- Executive Summary
- Meta Visual
- Presentation Slides
- Research Mapping
- Visual Language

Materials should support dialogue rather than dominate it.

---

# Expected Outcomes

A successful meeting should produce:

- shared understanding
- meaningful questions
- comparative observations
- potential collaboration topics
- inputs for future observation

Immediate agreement is not required.
