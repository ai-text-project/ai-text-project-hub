# AI Research Presentation Series

## Introduction

Thank you for your interest in the Human–AI Collaboration Research Program.

This directory contains the presentation materials prepared for AI research institutes, universities, and research organizations.

The objective of these presentations is not simply to explain completed research results.

Rather, they are intended to support **Research Comparative Dialogue**, enabling researchers to compare perspectives, exchange research experiences, and explore future possibilities for Human–AI Collaboration.

---

# Recommended Reading Order

The presentations are organized to be viewed sequentially.

We recommend reading them in the following order.

1. Visual 0 — Orientation
2. Visual 1 — Operational Challenge
3. Visual 2 — Operational Approach
4. Visual 3 — Operational Evidence
5. Visual 4 — Research Program
6. Visual 5 — Future Research Collaboration

Each presentation builds upon the previous one to gradually introduce the overall Research Program.

---

# Presentation Overview

The presentation series introduces:

- Operational challenges in long-term Human–AI Collaboration
- Research perspectives developed through the Research Program
- Comparative Dialogue as a collaborative research methodology
- Future opportunities for research collaboration

Rather than presenting a fixed methodology, the presentations describe an evolving research environment that continues to develop through dialogue and collaborative observation.
