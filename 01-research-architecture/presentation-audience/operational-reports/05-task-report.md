# Task Report for Harvest Strategist V2 (Provisional)

## Subject

Expansion of the Presentation Audience Portal Framework through the Development of an AI Research Edition

---

# Background

Following the successful completion of the Enterprise Presentation Audience Portal, a second audience-specific presentation was developed for AI research institutes and academic research communities.

The objective was not simply to prepare another presentation, but to evaluate whether the Presentation Audience concept could function as a reusable architectural framework across different audiences.

---

# Initial Observation

The Enterprise Presentation demonstrated that a Presentation could function as a Public Research Interface rather than merely a slide deck.

This raised a new question:

Can the same presentation architecture be adapted to different audiences while preserving the overall Research Program structure?

To explore this question, an AI Research Edition was designed.

---

# Development Process

Rather than creating a completely independent presentation, the existing Presentation Framework was reused.

The development process revealed a clear architectural separation.

## Shared Core

The following Visuals remained common across both editions.

- Visual 0 — Everyday Workplace Experience
- Visual 1 — Operational Challenge
- Visual 2 — Operational Approach
- Visual 3 — Collaborative Outcomes
- Visual 4 — Reflection and Insight
- Visual 9 — Visual Language Design

These Visuals introduce the fundamental philosophy of dialogue, collaborative understanding, and long-term Human–AI collaboration.

## Audience-Specific Layer

Visuals 5–8 were redesigned specifically for AI research audiences.

The resulting sequence became:

- Visual 5 — Future Collaborative Research
- Visual 6 — Research Mapping
- Visual 7 — Collaboration Framework
- Visual 8 — AI Research Ecosystem

Rather than focusing on enterprise collaboration, these Visuals describe the developmental process of collaborative research among researchers and research institutions.

---

# Structural Observation

An important structural distinction emerged.

The Presentation Framework consists of two complementary layers.

```text
Presentation Framework

↓

Common Core
(Visual 0–4, 9)

↓

Audience-Specific Layer
(Visual 5–8)
```

This observation suggests that the Presentation itself has evolved into a reusable architectural framework capable of supporting multiple audience-specific implementations.

---

# Developmental Interpretation

The AI Research Edition demonstrates that the Presentation Audience Portal is no longer a single presentation.

Instead, it functions as a Presentation Framework that can generate specialized editions while preserving a common narrative structure.

The current architecture can be interpreted as:

```text
Presentation Framework

↓

Enterprise Edition

↓

AI Research Edition
```

Future editions may be developed using the same framework for additional audiences without redesigning the overall architecture.

---

# Strategic Significance

This development provides several long-term advantages.

- Preservation of a common Research Program narrative.
- Audience-specific communication without architectural fragmentation.
- Reuse of Presentation assets across multiple domains.
- Reduced development effort for future editions.
- Consistent navigation throughout the Research Program.

The Presentation Audience Portal has therefore evolved from a single communication asset into a scalable communication architecture.

---

# Questions for Harvest Strategist V2

The following strategic questions are proposed.

1. Should the Presentation Framework become the standard architecture for future audience-specific communication?

2. Should Enterprise Edition and AI Research Edition be treated as the first two official implementations of this framework?

3. Which additional audiences would provide the greatest strategic value for future editions?

Examples may include:

- Universities

- Government research organizations
- International collaborative research projects
- Public education initiatives

4. How should the Presentation Framework be integrated into the long-term Enterprise Observation Campaign and external collaboration strategy?
