# Topology Preservation Protocol

## Purpose

This document records the operational protocol used to preserve structural topology during diagram generation.

Unlike the Structural Projection Workflow, which describes the overall creative process, this protocol defines the standardized operational procedures required to ensure consistent topology preservation.

The protocol is intended to function as a reusable operational asset for future diagram-generation tasks.

---

# Relationship to Structural Projection Workflow

The protocol builds upon the previously established Structural Projection Workflow.

The workflow describes how conceptual structures are transformed into diagrams.

The present protocol focuses on how topology preservation is operationally maintained during that process.

Accordingly:

```text
Structural Projection Workflow
        │
        ▼
Topology Preservation Protocol
```

The workflow provides the production process.

The protocol provides the operational quality-assurance procedures.

```text

Preparation

↓

Step 2
Structural Extraction

↓

Step 3
Topology Preservation Check

↓

Step 4
Compression Procedure

↓

Step 5
Validation Procedure

↓

Step 6
Final Output
```

Each operational step specifies:

- Objective
- Input
- Procedure
- Output

This standardized structure supports consistent execution across different diagram-generation tasks.

---

# Design Philosophy

The protocol intentionally separates operational execution from methodological discussion.

Its purpose is not to explain why topology preservation is important.

Instead, it defines how topology preservation is consistently implemented.

The design rationale is documented separately in the accompanying Design Memo.
