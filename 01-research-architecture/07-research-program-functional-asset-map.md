# Research Program Functional Asset Map

## Status

Provisional Functional Architecture

Research Program Structure

---

## Purpose

This document provides a functional overview of the major asset groups within the research program.

The assets are not classified by storage location or repository.

Instead, they are classified according to the functional role they play within the research program.

The purpose is to clarify the overall operational architecture of the research program.

---

## Functional Layers

### Layer A — Research Content

Assets describing observations, theories, and research outcomes.

Examples include:

* Flagship Papers
* Core Theory Papers
* Extended Theory Papers
* Case Assets
* Meta Case Assets
* Cluster Assets
* Research Notes

---

### Layer B — Research Infrastructure

Assets supporting preservation, navigation, and accessibility.

Examples include:

* GitHub Architecture
* Figure Assets
* Terminology System
* Concept Index
* Navigation System
* Repository Topology

---

### Layer C — Research Operations

Assets supporting research execution and operational continuity.

Examples include:

* Observation Protocols
* Evaluation Criteria
* Governance Assets
* Phase Control
* Role System
* Multi-Instance Operations

---

### Layer D — Research Mapping

Assets describing relationships among research structures.

Examples include:

* Research Maps
* Topology Maps
* Cluster Review Assets
* Meta Observation Assets
* Research-Space Evolution Assets
* Research Program Evolution Assets

---

### Layer E — Future Research

Assets describing unresolved directions and future development.

Examples include:

* Open Questions
* Candidate Clusters
* Candidate Meta Cases
* Future Research Directions
* Topology Hypotheses

---

## Functional Interpretation

The current research program may be viewed as a layered operational system.

```text
Research Content
        ↓
Research Infrastructure
        ↓
Research Operations
        ↓
Research Mapping
        ↓
Future Research
```

Each layer supports different functions within the overall research program.

The layers should be understood as complementary rather than hierarchical.

---

## Relationship to Repository Architecture

The functional asset map is independent of repository boundaries.

Individual assets may be distributed across multiple repositories while still belonging to the same functional layer.

Accordingly, this document should be interpreted as a research-program architecture rather than a repository map.
