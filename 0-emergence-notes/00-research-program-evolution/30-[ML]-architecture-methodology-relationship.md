## Purpose

This document records the current interpretation of the relationship between the Research Architecture and the Structural Observation Methodology.

Its purpose is not to introduce a new theoretical framework.

Instead, it documents how repeated methodological observations gradually revealed that architectural organization and methodological development represent two complementary dimensions of the research program.

This document should therefore be understood as a working interpretation within the ongoing formation history of the Methodological Layer.

---

## Background

Previous methodological documents progressively established individual components of the Structural Observation Methodology.

These included:

- Evaluation Framework
- Observation Criteria
- Observation Protocol
- Structural Observation Methodology
- Methodological Evolution Model

At the same time, repeated repository development and Research Mapping activities clarified the organizational structure of the Research Architecture.

Initially, these developments were interpreted independently.

However, recent observation suggested that they describe different aspects of the same research program.

---

## Current Observation

Current observations suggest that the Research Program contains two complementary organizational dimensions.

```text
Research Program

├── Research Architecture
│       (Structural Organization / Space)
│
└── Methodological Evolution
        (Developmental Organization / Time)
```

The Research Architecture primarily describes how research assets are organized.

The Methodological Evolution primarily describes how observational methodology develops through repeated application.

These two dimensions should be regarded as complementary rather than competing interpretations.

---

## Relationship between the Two Dimensions

The present interpretation is summarized as follows.

### Research Architecture

Focuses on structural organization.

Typical concerns include:

- repository organization
- research-space topology
- observation layers
- navigation
- architectural relationships

The architecture remains comparatively stable while supporting continued research activities.

---

### Methodological Evolution

Focuses on developmental refinement.

Typical concerns include:

- repeated structural observation
- comparative review
- methodological refinement
- protocol revision
- stabilization

Unlike the architecture itself, the methodology continues to evolve through accumulated observation.

---

## Working Interpretation

Current observations suggest that:

```text
Stable Architecture
        │
        ▼
Repeated Observation
        │
        ▼
Methodological Evolution
        │
        ▼
Improved Research Activities
        │
        ▼
Architectural Refinement
```

Accordingly, the architecture provides the structural environment within which methodological evolution occurs.

Conversely, methodological refinement may gradually influence future architectural organization.

The precise feedback mechanism remains under observation.

## Current Position

At present, the distinction between Architecture and Methodology should be regarded as an observational interpretation rather than a finalized theoretical claim.

Current evidence supports distinguishing:

- structural organization (Space)
- methodological development (Time)

Whether these two dimensions themselves belong to an even higher organizational framework remains an open research question.

---

## Future Observation

Future observations may clarify:

- how methodological evolution influences architectural organization,
- how architectural changes enable new methodological development,
- whether additional organizational dimensions emerge,
- whether a higher-order organizational framework should eventually be introduced.

These questions remain subjects for continued observation.
