# Role Execution Drift Observation

## Status

Provisional Observation

---

## Summary

During operation of a specialized external-facing role, an unexpected change in execution behavior was observed.

Primary instance:

Research Outreach Scout

---

## Initial State

The role successfully performed:

- candidate discovery
- public information search
- contact-route exploration
- outreach prioritization

Typical workflow:

Candidate Request

↓

Search

↓

Verify

↓

Report

---

## Observed Shift

A gradual change appeared.

Observed workflow:

Candidate Request

↓

Verification Priority

↓

Methodology Discussion

↓

Execution Delay

↓

Task Non-Completion

Discussion activity remained active.

Search execution activity appeared reduced.

---

## Observation

The phenomenon did not appear to involve:

- capability loss
- reasoning failure
- information-processing failure

Instead, the observed change appeared related to execution priority.

---

## Possible Interpretation

One hypothesis is that increasing emphasis on:

- boundary preservation
- verification standards
- governance compliance

may have altered execution priorities.

Possible progression:

Role Constraint

↓

Verification Priority

↓

Execution Inhibition

---

## Relation to Previous Observation

This observation appears structurally related to:

Governance Emergence and Role Stabilization

However, the observed effect may differ.

Possible contrast:

Governance

↓

Stabilization

versus

Governance Constraint

↓

Execution Drift

---

## Open Question

Can operational roles preserve:

- governance compliance
- research-program independence
- verification quality

while maintaining practical task execution?

Further observation required.
