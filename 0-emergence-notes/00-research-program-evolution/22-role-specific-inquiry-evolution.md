## Purpose

This document records the emergence of role-specific inquiry evolution as a distinct developmental stage within the research program.

The purpose is not to introduce a new theory.

Instead, it documents how operational roles may progressively reorganize their own inquiry while preserving the underlying research program.

## Developmental Background

Earlier stages of the research program primarily focused on:

* repository organization
* placement principles
* governance
* operational continuity
* historical validation

The present observation suggests an additional stage.

Rather than refining organizational structures, operational roles themselves began refining the questions through which they interpreted the research program.

---

## Typical Characteristics

Role-specific inquiry evolution may include:

* progressive reformulation of operational questions
* changing evaluation criteria
* reinterpretation of existing assets
* emergence of new operational viewpoints
* improved alignment between research assets and external adoption

The primary development occurs within the inquiry process rather than within the research framework.
