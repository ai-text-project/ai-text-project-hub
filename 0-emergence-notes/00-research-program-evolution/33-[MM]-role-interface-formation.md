# Role Interface Formation

## Status

Research Program Evolution

Provisional

---

# Purpose

This document records the provisional emergence of standardized role interfaces within the Research Program.

The objective is not to describe individual operational assets.

Instead, the document records how repeated role separation gradually led to explicit operational interfaces supporting collaboration between specialized research roles.

---

# Background

Earlier stages of the Research Program primarily focused on:

- observation,
- methodology formation,
- repository organization,
- operational standardization.

As individual research roles became increasingly specialized, stable interfaces between these roles also became necessary.

Recent observations suggest that these interfaces are beginning to emerge as reusable operational assets.

---

# Observed Development

Current observations suggest the following developmental progression:

```text
Role Separation
        │
        ▼
Specialized Research Roles
        │
        ▼
Operational Deliverables
        │
        ▼
Role Interfaces
        │
        ▼
Standardized Handover Assets
```

Rather than functioning as ordinary documentation, these assets support the consistent transfer of observational outcomes between independent research roles.

---

# Initial Observation

The first clearly documented example was the emergence of a standardized handover interface between:

```text
Cluster Reviewer
        │
        ▼
Paper Architect
```

The resulting handover memo established a stable operational boundary between comparative observation and manuscript development.

This observation suggests that role interfaces may themselves become reusable organizational assets.

---

# Interpretation

Current observations indicate that research maturation involves not only the specialization of individual roles but also the stabilization of the interfaces connecting those roles.

Accordingly, operational continuity may increasingly depend on standardized handover assets rather than on informal knowledge transfer.

This interpretation remains provisional and requires additional observations from other research roles.
