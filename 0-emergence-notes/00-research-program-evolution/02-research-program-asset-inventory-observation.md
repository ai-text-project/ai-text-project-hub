# Research Program Asset Inventory Observation

## Status

Provisional Observation

---

## Purpose

To document an observation concerning the emergence of research-program-level asset inventory practices.

This document does not evaluate the assets themselves.

Instead, it records the process through which accumulated research outputs became recognizable as components of a larger research program.

---

## Background

As the research program expanded, a growing number of assets had accumulated across multiple repositories and research layers.

Examples included:

* Papers
* Case observations
* Meta Cases
* Figures
* Research Maps
* Terminology systems
* Governance documents
* Operational procedures

Although these assets existed, they were primarily viewed through their individual functions.

No unified description of the overall asset structure existed.

As discussions concerning sustainability and future development increased, attention shifted toward understanding what had already been created.

---

## Observation

A proposal emerged to perform a systematic inventory of research assets.

Initially, assets were grouped according to visible categories such as:

* Paper Assets
* GitHub Assets
* Case Assets
* Figure Assets
* Methodology Assets
* Operational Know-How Assets

The inventory process began as a practical attempt to understand the scope of accumulated work.

However, the process produced a secondary effect.

The inventory itself became an observation mechanism.

Instead of revealing isolated assets, it revealed relationships among assets.

---

## Observed Change

During the inventory process, the perceived structure of the research program changed.

The focus moved from:

"What documents exist?"

to:

"What functions do these assets perform within the research program?"

This shift led to the emergence of a more functional classification structure.

Examples included:

* Research Content Assets
* Research Infrastructure Assets
* Research Operations Assets
* Research Mapping Assets
* Future Assets

As a result, the inventory evolved from a catalog of materials into a description of the research program itself.

---

## Significance

The significance of this observation was not the creation of a new repository or document.

Rather, it was the recognition that the research program possessed an identifiable asset structure.

The inventory process transformed previously separate outputs into visible components of a larger system.

In this sense, the inventory acted as a bridge between asset accumulation and program-level understanding.
