## Purpose

This document describes the operational procedure for evaluating topology preservation using the observation criteria established in:

Topology Preservation Observation Criteria.

The purpose is not to define new evaluation criteria.

Instead, this document provides a repeatable observation process that enables consistent structural comparison across different representations.

---

## Background

The preceding methodological framework established that topology preservation should be evaluated through structural observation rather than semantic similarity.

Accordingly, the next methodological requirement is to organize how those criteria should be applied during actual observation.

This document therefore operationalizes the observation methodology.

---

# Observation Procedure

## Step 1

Identify the observation target.

Examples include:

- diagrams
- workflow representations
- conceptual models
- repository organization
- methodological documents

---

## Step 2

Apply the Observation Criteria.

Evaluate each criterion independently.

Typical criteria include:

- Node
- Relation
- Flow
- Boundary
- Hierarchy

---

## Step 3

Identify structural invariants.

Determine whether comparable organizational structures remain preserved despite representational differences.

At this stage, semantic interpretation should be minimized.

---

## Step 4

Evaluate topology preservation.

Possible outcomes include:

- Preserved
- Partially Preserved
- Structurally Modified
- Not Comparable

The objective is descriptive evaluation rather than theoretical confirmation.

---

## Step 5

Record observations.

Document:

- observed invariants
- notable differences
- uncertainty
- limitations

These records provide material for future methodological refinement.

---

## Step 6

Review the protocol.

Repeated observations may reveal:

- additional observation criteria
- improved procedural ordering
- simplified evaluation
- new methodological requirements

Protocol revision should therefore occur only after sufficient observational evidence has accumulated.

---

# Operational Principles

The protocol follows several operational principles.

- Observe before interpreting.
- Compare organization before semantics.
- Preserve neutrality during evaluation.
- Separate observation from explanation.
- Record uncertainty explicitly.

# Scope

This protocol is intended for repeated structural observation across multiple representational formats.

Examples include:

- research papers
- diagrams
- repository topology
- workflow models
- conceptual architectures
- observation reports

The protocol is independent of domain-specific content and focuses exclusively on observable structural organization.
