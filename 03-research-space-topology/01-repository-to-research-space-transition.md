# Repository-to-Research-Space Transition

## Status

Provisional Observation

---

## Purpose

To document the observed transition from a repository-centered structure to a research-space-centered structure.

This document does not describe the formation of a research program.

Instead, it focuses on the emergence of an organized research space built upon repository infrastructure.

---

## Initial State

The project initially consisted of repositories containing:

- papers
- figures
- terminology
- notes
- governance documents

The primary concern was repository organization.

Typical questions included:

- Where should files be stored?
- How should repositories be related?
- How should navigation be structured?

The dominant perspective was repository management.

---

## Observed Transition

Over time, repositories ceased functioning solely as storage locations.

Instead, they began to function as components of a larger navigable environment.

Examples included:

- cross-repository navigation
- semantic projection pathways
- topology mapping
- repository relationship mapping
- emergence-note integration

The focus gradually shifted from:

Repository

↓

Storage

to:

Repository

↓

Navigation

↓

Research Space

---

## Emerging Characteristics

The emerging research space exhibited:

- semantic continuity across repositories
- navigational pathways between domains
- cross-reference structures
- topology-level organization
- repository-independent orientation

The space increasingly functioned as a coherent environment rather than a collection of repositories.

---

## Interpretation

A repository may be understood as a structural container.

A research space may be understood as an organized environment formed by relationships among multiple containers.

Possible progression:

Repository

↓

Navigation

↓

Topology

↓

Research Space

---

## Relation to Project Topology

This observation motivated the distinction between:

- Repository Topology
- Research Space Topology

Repository Topology focuses on:

- repository relations
- projection paths
- navigation structures

Research Space Topology focuses on:

- environment formation
- semantic continuity
- structural integration
- space-level organization

---

## Open Question

At what point does a collection of repositories become a research space?

Further observation required.
