## Purpose

This document provides a high-level architectural overview of the Structural Observation Methodology developed within the Research Space Topology layer.

The purpose is not to introduce new methodological concepts.

Instead, it clarifies how the individual methodological documents relate to one another and collectively support repeated structural observation.

---

## Background

As the methodological layer matured, several independent documents were developed to address different aspects of topology preservation.

Rather than functioning as isolated documents, these methodological components gradually formed an integrated observation architecture.

This document records that overall architecture.

---

# Functional Roles

Each document fulfills a distinct methodological function.

| Document | Primary Role |
|-----------|--------------|
| Topology Evaluation Framework | Establishes the overall evaluation philosophy |
| Observation Criteria | Defines observable structural categories |
| Observation Protocol | Defines the operational observation procedure |

Together, these documents form a coherent methodological workflow rather than independent references.

# Current Interpretation

Structural Observation Methodology should not be understood as a single protocol.

Instead, it represents an integrated methodological architecture consisting of:

- evaluation philosophy
- observation criteria
- operational protocol
- iterative refinement

The methodology therefore supports continuous improvement through repeated observation rather than fixed procedural execution.

---

# Future Development

Future development may include:

- refinement of observation criteria
- protocol validation through additional cases
- observer consistency studies
- visualization of methodological evolution
- integration with future methodological memoranda

Accordingly, this architecture should be regarded as an evolving methodological framework.
