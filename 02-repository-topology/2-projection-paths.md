# Projection Paths

This section organizes the major projection pathways across the AI Text Project ecosystem.

Rather than treating repositories as isolated destinations,
this layer describes how readers may move across different density levels and semantic environments.

The purpose of this layer is to support:

- multi-layer navigation
- reader-oriented entry pathways
- structure-preserving projection
- density-adaptive movement
- cross-repository continuity

---

## Main Projection Paths

### 1. Research → Bridge → Public

Typical pathway:

Dialogue-Phase-Reasoning
↓
AI-Textbook-Bridge
↓
Medium / Public Articles

Purpose:
- preserve structural continuity
- reduce density gradually
- improve external readability

---

### 2. Public → Bridge → Research

Entry pathway for external readers.

Typical flow:

Public Articles
↓
AI-Textbook-Bridge
↓
Dialogue-Phase-Reasoning

Purpose:
- lightweight entry
- gradual conceptual orientation
- topology-aware navigation

---

### 3. Topology-Oriented Navigation

Entry pathway for readers interested in:

- repository structures
- semantic topology
- projection systems
- navigation architectures

Typical flow:

Navigation Hub
↓
Project Topology
↓
Repository Relations
↓
Research Repositories

---

### 4. Researcher-Oriented Navigation

Entry pathway for readers seeking:

- flagship papers
- topology structures
- dialogue-phase models
- semantic tensor frameworks

Typical flow:

Flagship Papers
↓
Paper Maps
↓
Topology Structures
↓
Extended Research Layers

---

## Working Perspective

Projection paths are treated as structural navigation pathways rather than simple reading orders.

Different readers may enter the ecosystem through different density layers while maintaining semantic continuity across projections.

The goal is not maximal compression,
but stable movement across layered semantic environments.
